Render objects sit below the widget layer and give you direct access to Flutter's layout, painting, and hit-testing pipeline. These guides walk through each major concept in depth.

## In this section

- [Layout](/guides/layout) — How the layout phase works and what your render object must do during it.
- [Painting](/guides/painting) — Drawing to the canvas, pushing layers, and compositing children.
- [Hit Testing](/guides/hit-testing) — How pointer events are routed and how to claim them.
- [Semantics](/guides/semantics) — Exposing accessible meaning to screen readers and the widget inspector.
- [Compositing](/guides/compositing) — Working with the compositor layer tree for effects and transforms.
- [Diagnostics](/guides/diagnostics) — Surfacing debug information through the Flutter diagnostic system.
- [Mouse Cursors](/guides/mouse-cursors) — Changing the cursor shape when the pointer enters your render object.
- [Show on Screen](/guides/show-on-screen) — Requesting that a viewport scrolls to make this render object visible.
